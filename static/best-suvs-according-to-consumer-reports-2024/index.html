Best SUVs According to Consumer Reports 2024

You are here: Home / Uncategorized / Best SUVs According to Consumer Reports 2024

April 18, 2024 By Paolo Roxas
Categories: Uncategorized

The Best SUVs are always selling like hotcakes because they are fantastic all-around performers. You can take them on adventures, use them as a people or materiel hauler, and are fully capable of traversing more adverse terrain and weather. There is a sea of them out there, but not all are fantastic options for the money.

Thankfully, Consumer Reports has already selected the cream of the crop and created a list of valuable recommendations. Each vehicle went through a gauntlet of stringent tests which were created to test their capabilities and durability. CR does independent testing, meaning the vehicles were not sponsored in any way to avoid bias and pressure.

There are no specific brands when CR rounded up the machines they would test, but the results speak for themselves. All of the best car brands have at least one representative in this lineup. Their track records are proven by both tests and owner satisfaction ratings, so you can’t go wrong with either one.

Best SUVs by Consumer Reports 2024

Subcompact SUVs

Subcompact SUVs are like upgraded sedans that have better ride height, space, and overall capacity. They are popular simply because they are affordable AWD machines that a young professional or student can take on weekend adventures or grocery shopping.

The top three picks hear are popular because of their fantastic pricing and legendary reliability. The Subaru Crosstrek is a well-known adventure-ready subcompact, while the Corolla Cross and HR-V are some of the most practical and reliable cars in the market.

Compact SUVs

Compact SUVs own the road everywhere since there are more and more of them plying our streets and freeways every day. They offer more room and comfort for a small family, plus some luggage or big items in the back.

The elusive yet highly desirable RAV4 Prime leads the pack with its upgraded interior, aesthetics, and efficiency. The Subaru Forester is quite popular with every type of driver, including the adventurous ones, while the Kia Sportage upgrades its lineage with plug-in hybrid capabilities.

Midsize SUVs

Midsize SUVs are more ideal for road trips and vacations since they offer more trunk space, a more powerful drivetrain, and a slightly longer wheelbase. A family of 5 would be more comfortable here versus the category above due to more legroom and width.

The Subaru Outback takes the top spot this year but is closely followed by the spacious Ford Edge and the svelte Venza. All three are fantastic dailies and closely tied, but the Outback’s offroad ability gives it the edge.

The hybrid Highlander is very hard to find because of its wild popularity despite the inception of the larger Grand Highlander. Its closely tailed by the equally popular Telluride and the Palisade, both of which also fly off the dealer’s lots as soon as they drop them from the trailer truck.
